## 🎯 Assignment Objectives & Methodology

The core philosophy of this assignment is that *you cannot improve a workflow you have never mapped.* The objectives achieved in this submission include:

1. **Workflow Mapping:** Identifying 10-15 real recurring tasks from my study, work, and side projects.
2. **AI Categorization:** Establishing a baseline for where AI is beneficial and where it is a liability. Every task is categorized into one of four buckets:
   - **Just me:** Tasks requiring human context, empathy, or deep learning.
   - **Delegate to AI with review:** Repetitive tasks that require human QA.
   - **Collaborate with AI:** Ideation, debugging, and brainstorming.
   - **Fully automate:** Trivial data parsing and formatting.
3. **Defining Success:** Selecting three specific "target tasks" that will be reused in future phases (FL-02 through FL-04), complete with strict, measurable "Done Well" success definitions.
4. **Environment Configuration:** Creating a structured Claude Project workspace complete with specific custom instructions (Tone, Goals, Persona) to steer the AI's behavior.

## 📝 Notes for the Reviewer

Here is some additional context regarding my methodology for this assignment:

- **Authenticity & Context:** All tasks listed in the audit are drawn directly from my actual weekly routine as a software developer/student, avoiding generic examples. 
- **The "Just Me" Rule:** I have explicitly flagged tasks like *Reviewing Peer Pull Requests* and *Reading Technical Documentation* as "Just Me." While AI can summarize these, outsourcing them prevents me from building my own mental models and team trust, which are critical for my long-term technical growth.
- **Custom Instructions Strategy:** My Claude setup explicitly demands concise, technical responses that explain the *why* behind concepts, rather than just generating copy-paste code. This ensures the AI acts as a mentor rather than a crutch.
- **Tooling Setup:** My foundational toolkit (Claude, ChatGPT, and Anthropic Academy) is fully configured, and the required introductory modules have been completed.
